About Me

Professionally

In spring 2023, I graduated Summa Cum Laude from Siena College with a B.S. Degree. In undergraduate, I double majored in Physics and Applied Data Science and obtained minors in Astronomy and Mathematics. During these four years, I also discovered my strong interests in astronomy, big data, data science, and programming. Throughout undergraduate, I explored a wide variety of research interests including working with the Dark Energy Spectroscopic Instrument, Berkeley Search for Extraterrestrial Intelligence, and The University of Hawaii Institute for Astronomy. See my CV for more details.

In Fall 2023, I will applying these interests as an Astronomy graduate student at the University of Arizona Department of Astronomy ( Steward Observatory). I am motivated to study astronomy by the possibilities to unravel deep mysteries of the universe. One area I am especially interested in is understanding black holes. As a result, I plan to study transients like tidal disruption events which can provide important insight into the area surrounding these mysterious objects. (Disclaimer: this is my interest as of Summer 2023 but is subject to change since I'm only a first year!)

Hobbies

In my spare time I pursue many (probably too many) hobbies. To list a few, I play ultimate frisbee, hike and backpack, alpine ski, and mountain bike. I have played Ultimate Frisbee my first year of high school where I started with Air Traffic Control at Brighton High School. Then, I played with Siena College's team through undergraduate. I've mostly hiked and backpacked along the east coast, primarly in New York's Adirondack State Park. Similarly, I have spent most of my time skiing and mountain biking on the east coast but am very excited to spend the next chapter of my life exploring the southwest US!

These athletic activities take up most of my time but I also like to travel and take photos, especially landscapes. In fact, the background of the home page of this website is from a backpacking trip I took in the High Peaks Region of the Adirondacks!
